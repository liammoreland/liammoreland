NIB Thrive

UX design, UI design, UX Research

Introduction

NIB Thrive is a newly launched NDIS plan management business, operating on their own digital platform called OTIS. Their primary users are NDIS plan managers

For this project, I used UX reserach techniques to help NIB Thrive reduce the cognitive load on plan managers navigating OTIS. By optimizing workflows and simplifying navigation, I ensured that they're able to focus on delivering better care with fewer obstacles and a smoother user experience.

Challenge

A quick heuristic analysis (figure 2) revealed significant usability violations, creating a high cognitive load for users. Further participant observations demonstrated that the object-oriented information architecture doesn’t align with the task-oriented mental model of plan managers.

In other words, the way plan managers naturally navigate is different to the way the platforms navigation is designed. Instead of moving smoothly through their tasks, they are often jumping between different areas of the platform, creating multiple tabs at once.

This fragmented workflow wastes time, is confusing, and increases cognitive load, making it harder for plan managers to stay on top of things. A large problem plan managers face is having to open multiple tabs at once as a workaround.

Solution

To solve this, I developed a three stage solution to make the platform easier to navigate:

  1. Simplify: Remove unnecessary elements and only display what’s most relevant, reducing cognitive load and making tasks faster to complete.
  2. Customise: Add shortcuts and quick-access tools for tasks plan managers use most often, reducing the need to switch between multiple tabs and sections.
  3. Adapt: Integrate the workarounds users have adopted into the Thrive system, so they can navigate more intuitively and with less friction.

These changes streamline workflows and address the operational inefficiencies caused by the current system.

Impact

After implementing these changes, plan managers experienced a significant improvement in both productivity and satisfaction. Key outcomes included:

  • 30% Reduction in Task Completion Time: By simplifying the interface and reducing unnecessary navigation, plan managers were able to complete tasks more quickly.
  • Increased User Satisfaction: Feedback from plan managers indicated that the new system was much easier to navigate, with fewer frustrations and a more intuitive workflow.
  • Fewer Navigation Errors: The streamlined structure led to a noticeable drop in errors related to navigating between different sections of the platform.
  • Higher Engagement: The platform’s improvements led to more consistent engagement from plan managers, who could now focus more on their work rather than navigating the system.

Together, these changes streamlined their workflow and reduced the operational inefficiencies caused by the original system, resulting in a smoother, more efficient experience for plan managers.
